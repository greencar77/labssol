Here are some examples of phrasal verbs, including separable phrasal verbs where words appear between the verb and the particle.

===No words in between
She looked after her younger brother.
We ran into an old friend at the mall.
They came across an interesting article online.
I gave up smoking last year.
He woke up early this morning.
The plane took off on time.
We checked in at the hotel.
She got over her illness quickly.

===Words in between (separable phrasal verbs)
She turned the light off before leaving.
He picked his daughter up from school.
I looked the word up in the dictionary.
They called the meeting off because of the storm.
Please write your phone number down.
We put the boxes away after moving.
She handed the report in yesterday.
The teacher gave the homework back today.
He filled the form out carefully.
I threw the old magazines away.

===Pronouns between the verb and particle

When the object is a pronoun, it usually must go between the verb and the particle.

She picked him up after work.
Please turn it off.
I looked it up online.
They called it off.
Don't throw them away.
He put it on immediately.
Please take it back.
She gave it back yesterday.

===Longer examples
After dinner, I looked the recipe up and wrote it down.
The manager called the entire event off because of the weather.
She picked her younger brother up from soccer practice.
We ran into our former teacher while we were shopping.
He turned the television off, picked the remote up, and put it away.
The librarian looked the title up in the catalog and wrote the location down for me.
I took my jacket off because the room was warm, and later I put it back on when it got cold.
They set the meeting up last week but called it off the next day.

These examples demonstrate both inseparable phrasal verbs (e.g., look after, run into, come across) and separable phrasal verbs (e.g., turn off, pick up, look up, call off), including the important rule that pronoun objects normally go between the verb and the particle.
